WW2 - 1943

The Eastern front continues

With the momentum and initiative, the Red Army would continue from its successes and push the Germans all the way to its starting position before the summer campaign. The Germans however, still had the ability to attack and so it did and created a salient around Kursk. It was here that occurred the largest tank battle in history with more than 2000 tanks participating (2000 from the Soviets). The Germans nearly won but with the Allied invasion of Italy, they had to divert troops to defend their ally. From here, the Soviets would be on the offensive.

Allied invasion of Italy

The Allies had two plans: invade from the South or through France. They first chose the South because of the recent victories in Africa. The Italians overthrew the current prime minister Benito Mussolini and made an armistice with the Allies. In turn, the Germans disarmed and invaded Italy and saved Mussolini. The Allies were able to push, barely reaching Rome due to bad weather. With winter coming, the battles came to a halt and it mainly went into stalemate.
